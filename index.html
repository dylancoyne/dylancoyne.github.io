About me

I have always enjoyed making and crafting things. I won a prize for one of my first school projects by creating a Celtic hill fort out of mud and straw, an old cereal box and lollipop sticks. At University I studied a course that covered the breadth of digital media, design and culture where I focused my learnings on the value of user-centred design.

What I do

I started my career in digital over 12 years ago and I have learned from experience that planning, strategy and teamwork are key ingredients to delivering successful projects. Prioritising workloads, defining processes, managing teams and mentoring juniors, I ensure the quality of all design output brings stories and brands to life.

Shaping Design

To create the best experience for the user I research the project, consider the pros and cons of different approaches, formulate a plan and devise a strategy. This forms a solid base for concept development and effective design implementation.

Hands on design

My passion for digital design is helped by a rather large chunk of skills in the following: Adobe Creative Suite, UI design methodologies, Wireframing, Concepting, Prototyping, Responsive Design, HTML, HTML5, CSS, Testing, Animation, Video Editing.

Summer of Penguin

Concepts, Design, Art Direction

As Lead Designer I was tasked with creating a new website for Penguin offering London Underground passengers access to free book extracts and author interviews as part of a campaign to celebrate its 80th birthday. Throughout the month of August, users could read exclusive excerpts from novels, as well as author interviews and audio content at summerofpenguin.com. The site was launched in partnership with TfL and Virgin Media. New content was added daily but some of the content highlights were an extract from Nick Hornby's book Funny Girl and one from Stephen Fry's forthcoming memoir More Fool Me. There was also a page of notes from one of Raymond Chandler's notebooks.

The colours chosen loosely reference different Tube lines and the icons to accompany each extract were designed by Rob Lowe. At the end of each excerpt is a cover shot of the book and a link to buy the publication online.

My responsibilities also included Art Directing marketing collateral across the Virgin Media Wi-Fi portal and the TfL Plan a Journey page.

Project completed for Penguin Random House. Website copyright Penguin Random House. Illustrations by Rob Lowe. Development by Flockler.

  • Client: Penguin Random House

Christopher Fowler

Concepts, UX, Design, Art Direction, Branding
