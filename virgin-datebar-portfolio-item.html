Virgin Holidays

Redefining search for the future of travel

Collaborating closely with key stakeholders, product owners and developers in an Agile project managed team I delivered innovative new features such as bookable multi-destination holidays online. I was also in a position as an integral part of the design team to help foster a user-centered design approach at Virgin Holidays.

The digital team were in their infancy and I was brought in to help organise the digital offering of PRH from creative campaigns to digital design strategy, creating digital guidelines and help with the digital delivery - no mean feat! I pretty quickly worked out that collaboration was going to be key for this role.

Moreover, there were countless other projects that required my design and creative input. From milestone celebrations like Summer of Penguin, to collaborating across departments on consumer campaigns and facilitating creative workshops. Highlight initiatives such as growing vertical strategies such as Happy Foodie and Dead Good Books. These were amongst some of my most interesting projects to work on.

When I needed to come up with a campaign for so and so’s latest book. Where did I start? Firstly, I would spend some time researching the subject to see if anything had come before. And then setup a creative workshop with a few key members of the team to sound out different ideas. I then would think about who would be the audience we are targeting with this digital design or campaign.

I then would move on to roughly sketching my ideas out on paper. This section of the process is really important to get any ideas in your head onto a page and see what works and what doesn't. These sketches get more and more refined before iterating and moving to final designs.

One of my favourite campaigns was the summer of penguin campaign to celebrate Penguins 80th birthday. Involving various 3rd parties, illustrators, creatives, developers and agencies.

Penguin collaborated with Transport for London and Virgin Media’s WiFi service on a mobile-first Summer of Penguin site, where passengers could choose daily excerpts of new books, ideas and author interviews during the summer. “This partnership takes the written word beyond the page to inspire and entertain travellers on the London Underground and celebrates the stories, writing and ideas that have shaped Penguin over the past eighty years,” said Hannah Telfer, group director, Audiences at Penguin Random House UK, in the DRUM The promotion, which ran from August 3-28 2016, was the perfect vehicle to showcase content from an author roster that inluded Harper Lee, John Cleese and Terry Pratchett, and bring those works to a greater audience.
